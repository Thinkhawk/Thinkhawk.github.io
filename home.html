| COLLABORATORS

As we all know the present scenario of the entire world seems to be very tragic due to the outbreak of the Novel Coronavirus. In this situation the entire humankind is going through a very pathetic phase in all possible ways. People are starving without food and a whole lot of them are totally clueless about the gravity of the situation. People are not only being afffected by the Virus but also in many other ways. There is a shortage of basic daily needs due to the break in the supply chain which includes food, medicine etc. Thus we have collaborated with ForHumanity to help our fellow beings out there whose day to day lifestyle has changed due to all the above reasons. Our main objectives are to identify people who are struggling to get basic amenities (like food and medicine) and reporting and reacting to the same, spreading positivity and to make sure that voiceless creatures around us aren't left to starve.

| FOUNDERS

Arundhathy Ravi is the Board Chair of UDHAVU India. She has held different leadership positions both within the government and the private sector. She served with the government as an Indian Administrative Service officer in various sectors including rural and area development, health, commerce, shipping and ports. She is presently associated with Boards of several companies, non-profit organizations and associations.

Revanth is the Director of the Accountability Initiative (AI) at the Centre for Policy Research. AI is a research group that focuses on tracking government planning, decision-making systems in key social sector programs, with a view to strengthen public debate on the issues of governance, institutions and accountability for public services. He has extensive research and experience on issues related to strengthening citizen participation in public services.

Syed Nameer Ahmed is the Executive Director of UDHAVU India. Syed is based in New Delhi and provides technical, administrative, financial and strategic oversight to the 100+ staff working pan-India on 40+ ongoing research projects and multi-sector policy and training engagements. Syed worked at Pratham, a pan-India non-profit initiative working towards universal quality primary education to ensure every child is in school and learning well. Syed has extensive research and evaluation experience in managing large scale surveys.

Chandralekha serves full time as Chief Executive of UDHAVU India. She is the founder of Association of Third Sector Researchers in India, Resource Mobilization Network in India, Campaign for Human Rights (Dalits and Tribals), and CAF India. She is a Member of the NGO Accreditation Committee of the Planning Commission, Member of Grants Approval Committee of the Ministry of Social Justice & Empowerment, Member of the Steering Group of the Eleventh Five Year Plan.
